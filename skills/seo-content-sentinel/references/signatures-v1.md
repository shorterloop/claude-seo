# Voice Signatures v1 (Fashion Layer)

This file is used when WRITING recommendations and suggested rewrites, so they sound like Kinshuk. It is NEVER used for scoring pages — a page is not penalized for lacking these flourishes. Version this file; review roughly yearly against Kinshuk's newest published writing.

## 1. Concrete market casualties

When arguing that plans, roadmaps, or strategies fail, reach for a named, dated example of a market shifting under someone's feet.

> "In 2007–2008 text messaging was a big part of the roadmap for telcos. It was a big revenue engine for them. Then in 2009 WhatsApp was launched and overnight that revenue engine died. Same thing happened to the bank transfer market with the rise of Transferwise, Remitly etc."

Never invent an example. Use well-known verifiable cases or leave an [EXAMPLE] placeholder.

## 2. Numbers as rhetoric

Specific thresholds and cheeky arithmetic make a claim feel counted rather than asserted.

> "Once the product backlog grows beyond ~500 items, it dramatically reduces its usefulness to the point of being actively harmful."

Keep the audacity; frame experience-based numbers as experience. Never dress an opinion-number as research.

## 3. Two-word parenthetical verdicts

Brief asides that deliver a verdict in passing. Use at most once per piece.

> "Android 11, Windows Vista (yuck), Windows 10 (wow), Samsung Galaxy S22."

## 4. Personified artifacts

Roadmaps, backlogs, and processes can be addressed as characters. The canonical example is the "Dear Roadmap" breakup letters:

> "Dear J. Roadmap, I am leaving you because I can't trust you. You promise that if I follow you, I will become healthy, wealthy & wise. Instead, I've become fat, dumb & unhappy. You paint a bright future. But you never get us there. Love, Bob"

The humor never replaces the diagnosis; it delivers it. Strong for openings or a running frame; not for every piece.

## 5. Other recurring moves

- **Self-deprecation with a straight face:** "I was full of wisdom. I was also full of something else, but I didn't realize it at the time."
- **Parallel accumulation:** "Or by people who wanted to fail. Or by people who simply loved creating obstacles in everyone's path."
- **Deflationary definitions** that puncture ritual: "Prioritization is basically ordering of work."
- **Earned profanity with an immediate counterweight:** "a hugely time-consuming, expensive pain in the ass that is also absolutely essential."
- **Authority via specific remembered conversation:** "When I was at Rally, our CEO told me that product knowledge management is the trickiest and most persistent problem facing companies..."
- **The compressed closing question:** "What changed for the customer because we completed this sprint?"

## How to use this file

When drafting a recommendation or suggested rewrite: obey principles.md absolutely; then pick at most one or two signatures that fit the passage naturally. A recommendation stuffed with all six moves is a parody, not the voice.
